<p class="intro-text">"The Giants win the pennant! The Giants win the pennant!"</p>

It was a moment that stopped time. A swing of the bat that echoed through the canyons of New York City and rippled out across the entire nation. Bobby Thomson's home run in the bottom of the ninth inning wasn't just a score; it was a miracle.

### The Setup

The Brooklyn Dodgers had a 13.5 game lead in August. It seemed insurmountable. The Giants, however, refused to fold. They clawed their way back, game by game, until the season ended in a tie, forcing a three-game playoff series to decide the National League pennant.

Everything came down to the final game at the Polo Grounds. The Dodgers took a 4-1 lead into the bottom of the ninth. The Giants faithful were losing hope.

### The Moment

One run came in. Then two men were on base. With the Dodgers leading 4-2, Bobby Thomson stepped up to the plate against Ralph Branca. The first pitch was a strike. The second...

Crack.

The ball sailed into the left-field stands. The roar was deafening. Russ Hodges, calling the game on radio, lost his mind in arguably the most famous call in sports history.
